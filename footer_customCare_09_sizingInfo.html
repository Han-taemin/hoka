Footwear sizing guide If you are unsure which shoe size to purchase, please follow the following steps:

  1. Draw a straight line, longer than your foot, on a piece of paper.
  2. Place the paper on a flat surface. Stand on the line with your heel and longest toe centered on the line. If measuring a child's foot, it may be easier to hold the paper up to their foot.
  3. Place a mark on the line at the tip of the longest toe and at the back of the heel.
  4. Repeat steps 1-3 for the other foot.
  5. Measure the distance between the marks. Taking the larger of the two measurements, use the conversion chart to find your correct shoe size.
All gender styles: sizing and FAQs What is the width?:

Traditionally, Women's sizes have been built as a B width and Men's as a D width. For All-Gender Styles, these tend to have more of a C/D width to be able to fit most feet. The good news is our adjustable straps help accommodate a wide range of foot widths and sizes!

What does "All Gender" mean?:

While traditionally footwear has been made based on Men's/Women's gender, we are beginning to look at fit differently when it comes to width, length, and volume, regardless of gender. By removing gender constructs, you are opening the style up to be worn by whoever likes the shoe/color and fit regardless of gender.
