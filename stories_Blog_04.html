    GAINING CONTROL BY LETTING GO

    One of the first steps toward regaining some sense of control is acknowledging that you aren't in control — that the emotions you're feeling stem from social and biological forces. You're still the same wonderful you and wherever you're at in your life is completely okay. Easier said than done? Absolutely. To help you reflect on these realities with good self-talk, we've put together a starter list of affirmations and journal prompts. First, a few tips!

    SELF-CARE JOURNALING TIPS
    1. Consistency is key. Just like any exercise, consistency is what will create lasting change. Pick a time and routine that works for you: five minutes a day, ten minutes 3x a week — whatever you can stick with and doesn't feel immediately overwhelming.
    2. Tack it on to another habit. One of the best ways to form a new habit is to link it with another habit you've already cemented in your daily routine. Team it with your morning coffee, skincare ritual, post-workout stretches or evening bath. Whatever feels good and natural to you.
    3. Choose a format you enjoy. Not everyone loves putting pen to paper. If that works for you, great! You can also type it out, turn it into a video journal, or make use of your phone's voice note app. There are no rules — aside from being kind and gentle with yourself.

    AFFIRMATIONS & JOURNAL PROMPTS
    1. What are 15 of your best qualities? 15 may seem like a lot, but getting beyond the first two or three that spring to mind helps you get specific and realize the many unique ways in which you elevate your community.
    2. Five things you're grateful for. Clinical studies have shown that simply keeping a gratitude journal benefits mood, sleep, blood pressure, immune function, and long-term mental health, building up your resilience to the seemingly insurmountable.
    3. When have you've shown resilience in the past? “It gets better” can sound a little trite when you're stuck in the doldrums giving yourself specific examples of times it has, indeed, gotten better can boost your confidence and strengthen your reserves in the darker moments.
    4. Think of a time you felt deeply at peace. Write every detail. Getting all your senses involved can quickly shift the mood of the day.
    5. Describe your morning routine. Are you happy with it? Is there anything you could add or edit to kick things off on the right foot?
