JAN 26, 2023Discover Chicago's Best Running Trails

Running in a city doesn't have to mean endless views of high-rise buildings and dodging pedestrians on crowded sidewalks. Knowing the right routes allows you to enjoy iconic landmarks and discover hidden treasures.

  • Models featured wearing the Trail Zest Pack

    Lakefront Trail: 17.6mi point-to-point
    • -Skill level: Easy
    • -Neighborhood: Runs as far north as Edgewater to as far south as South Shore
    • -Other notes: Kids, dogs and bikes allowed. Paved trail.

    The Lakefront Trail is the most popular in Chicago. As you run along Lake Michigan, you'll pass several iconic Chicago landmarks, including Navy Pier, Monroe Harbor, North Avenue Beach and the Museum District. You can begin your run from any of the multiple access points along the route. Plus, there's beach access and lots of waterfront restaurants to grab a quick bite, as well as many benches and picnic tables along the route if you need a break.

  • Models featured wearing the Trail Zest Pack

    Chicago Riverwalk: 2.7mi out-and-back
    • -Skill level: Easy
    • -Neighborhood: Downtown/Loop
    • -Other notes: Kids and dogs allowed. Bikes are allowed on part of the trail read signs to know where to walk your bike. Paved trail.

    This trail is perfect for those seeking stunning views of the city's skyscrapers. There are also many people-watching opportunities as you pass boat tours and people kayaking on the river. For a long break, check out one of the many bars and restaurants along the riverwalk. You should make note that casual walkers and commuters share the Riverwalk Trail with runners, so it can get crowded at certain points of the day.

  • Models featured wearing the Trail Zest Pack

    The 606 (Bloomingdale Trail): 5.5mi out-and-back
    • -Skill level: Easy
    • -Neighborhood: Humboldt Park, Logan Square, Bucktown and Wicker Park
    • -Other notes: Kids, dogs and bikes allowed. Paved trail.

    Built along a former rail line, this urban greenway features public art and landscaping along its 5.5-mile trail. You'll see a chain of street-level parks from your elevated vantage point and many scenic look-out points along the way. You can access the trail from different points, including the Exelon Observatory, Julia de Burgos Park, Damen Arts Plaza, Park No. 567 and Walsh Park.

  • Models featured wearing the Trail Zest Pack

    Montrose Harbor Trail: 5.4mi out-and-back

    • -Skill level: Easy
    • -Neighborhood: near Nothalsted and Buena Park, but mostly along the Montrose Harbor
    • -Other notes: Kids, dogs and bikes allowed. Most of the trail is paved, but a few areas are unpaved.

    If you're looking for a walk or run along the water, look no further than the Montrose Harbor Trail. Generally considered an easy route, it's perfect for soaking in the stunning views of Montrose Harbor. Many people love this route for birding, hiking and road biking, so you're likely to encounter other people while exploring. The trail is open year-round and is beautiful to visit anytime.
